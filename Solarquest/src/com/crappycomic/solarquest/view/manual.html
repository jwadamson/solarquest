Solarquest

Solarquest is a game of real estate market domination set in our Solar System. Players compete with each other to travel between the various planets and their moons, buying and consolidating properties along the way.

Setup

Each player starts on {start_node} with:

  • {initial_cash} Federons
  • {initial_fuel} hydrons of fuel
  • {initial_fuel_stations} fuel stations

The order in with the players play is randomized when the game starts.

The Board

{start_node}
Home of the Federation League headquarters. Players that pass {start_node} receive {pass_start_cash} Federons from the Federation League. Players that land on {start_node} receive {land_on_start_cash} Federons. Fuel is available on {start_node} for {fuel_price_on_start} Federons per hydron.
Planets and Moons
Rocky objects in the Solar System can be landed upon. Since they have gravity, players must expend fuel in order to blast off of their surfaces. Most planets and moons can be bought, sold, and exploited for profit. Players charge each other fees for landing on planets and moons that they own. Players may also place fuel stations in order to provide fuel to weary travelers.
Blue Dots
Interplanetary and orbital space is shown as blue dots on the board. These spaces have no effect on players' spaceships.
Black dots
Most planets are massive enough to generate a gravity well. These show up as black dots on the board. Players may never land on these spaces. If a player is in orbit and has not rolled a number high enough to pass the last black dot when leaving orbit, the player must continue to orbit. If a player is not in orbit and makes a roll that would land him or her on a black dot, he or she is pulled backward until he or she can land.
Federation Stations
The Federation League operates several outposts throughout the Solar System. Players that land receive a bonus from the Federation League.
Space Docks
Fuel is such a vital resource that entrepreneurs have built space docks at strategic points in the Solar System. Players docking at space docks may be required to pay a docking fee, but may purchase fuel after doing so.
Research Labs
There's always science to be had somewhere in the Solar System. Players can purchase controlling shares in research labs and then charge other players laboratory fees when they visit. No fuel is available on research labs, though.

Property

Players may purchase planets, moons, space docks, and research labs that they land on ({start_node} may never be purchased). Players that land on an owned property must pay the owner for the privilege. The amount of money increases if the owner owns more properties in the same color group. It never costs anything for a player to land on a property he or she owns or on a property that belongs to the Federation League.

Players may sell unwanted properties back to the Federation League {node_buyback_availability}. Players may sell properties to settle a debt owed to another player. The player receives the full price on the deed, plus {fuel_station_cost}, if a fuel station is present.

Fuel and Fuel Stations

Players expend fuel every time they blast off from a planet or moon. If a player is on a planet or moon and rolls a number higher than the number of hydrons of fuel in his or her tank, the player does not move. If the player is on a property owned by another player, he or she must again pay the owner rent for remaining on the property for an extra turn.

Players may refuel at planets or moons on which a fuel station has been placed or at any space dock. If the property is owned, the player pays the owner the fuel price indicated on the deed. If the player owns multiple properties of the same color, the fuel price goes up. Refueling at a property that is owned by the Federation League is {fuel_available_on_unowned_node}. When allowed, the fuel price on an unowned property is the lowest price listed on the deed. Players can refuel to a maximum of {maximum_fuel} hydrons.

To avoid becoming stuck on a planet or moon, players may want to refuel only to {low_fuel} hydrons, that being the greatest number that can be rolled that does not result in drawing a Red Shift card. If a player with {low_fuel} or fewer hydrons of fuel lands on an owned planet or moon that lacks a fuel station, the player may declare the owner negligent and purchase the property for the full price indicated on the deed.

Certain Red Shift cards also cause the player to expend fuel. If a Red Shift card requires more fuel than is in a player's tank, that player immediately loses the game.

Players that start a turn on a planet or moon with fewer than {minimum_fuel} hydrons of fuel may not roll the dice without first refueling to at least {minimum_fuel} hydrons. If the player cannot refuel, he or she has become stranded and immediately loses the game.

When on {start_node}, fuel is available for {fuel_price_on_start} Federons per hydron.

Players may place a fuel station on a planet or moon immediately upon purchasing it or when landing on it on a subsequent turn. Once placed, fuel stations never move, even when the property later changes hands. Fuel stations are worth {fuel_station_price} Federons at all times. They raise the deed price of properties, when present. They can be purchased from the Federation League {fuel_station_purchase_availability}. They can be sold to the Federation League {fuel_station_buyback_availability}. They can be sold to the Federation League to settle a debt owed to another player. They may never be traded between players.

The Federation League has a supply of {total_fuel_stations} fuel stations. Players can buy up all remaining fuel stations in order to withhold them from other players. This makes it more likely that other players will run low on fuel and possibly become stranded.

Red Shift Cards

When a player rolls {red_shift_roll}, he or she does not move, but instead draws a Red Shift card and follows the directions shown.

Laser Battles

Under the current rules, laser battles are {laser_battles_allowed}.

When allowed by the current rules, players may attack each other with ship-to-ship lasers. Players may attack before refueling and before rolling the dice or after moving, but before paying rent.

Players may attack ships up to {laser_battle_maximum_distance} spaces away, including ships on the same space as the player. Lasers draw a lot of power, and use more power over greater distances. This consumes fuel at the rate of {laser_battle_fuel_cost} hydrons per space of distance (plus one, so attacking ships on the same space costs {laser_battle_fuel_cost} hydrons). Players can fire at multiple ships on the same space at once for no additional fuel cost.

During an attack, the attacker rolls the dice. If the attacker rolls doubles, the shot has hit and damaged the target ship(s). Each player that suffers damage must pay the attacker {laser_battle_damage_cost} Federons for each pip on the dice. If the attacker rolls double {die_pips}, the fully powered laser destroys the ship(s) that it hits. The players, without their ships, immediately surrender their possessions to the attacker and are out of the game. If the attacker does not roll doubles, the shot misses with no ill effects and play continues.

Attacking from {start_node} is {lasers_can_fire_from_start}. Attacking a player who is on {start_node} is {lasers_can_fire_at_start}.

Winning the Game

The last player standing is the winner. Alternately, players can agree on a time limit. In time limit games, the player with the greatest net worth is the winner.

Losing the Game

Players can be eliminated from the game in the following ways:

  • Declaring bankruptcy when faced with an insurmountable debt.
  • Becoming stranded by attempting to roll the dice while on a planet or moon with fewer than {minimum_fuel} hydrons of fuel.
  • Drawing a Red Shift card that requires the use of more fuel than the player has available.
  • Being destroyed by a fully powered laser shot, in games where laser battles are allowed.

When declaring bankruptcy, everything the player owned becomes the property of the creditor. In the other two cases, everything the player owned becomes the property of the Federation League. All fuel stations that were placed on properties remain where they are.
